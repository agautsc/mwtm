Meals with the Mevans

Introduction

For years I have prepared for this day. I remember this first time I heard Meg and Evan were splitting car insurance. We were sitting at The Handlebar, at the table right next to the bar and side window, eating fries and drinking Trifecta. To them, the fact of shared insurance came out nonchalantly — like Evan mentioning a new sickness. For me, it seemed a sign of a legal, lifetime bond. I dunked my fingers in ice water, flicked drops of water on the couple, made the sign of the cross, and proclaimed them married.

Right after the Mevans’ first “marriage” I started to think of the story I’d tell at their official wedding: when, in college, Evan first mentioned Meg to me; the beginning of Mevan 2.0; and, of course, Evan’s famous trip to Monterrey’s were all on the list. These are all good stories, but nothing better explains the Mevans’ importance in all our lives than the meals we shared with them.

Besides immediate family, there is no doubt that I’ve shared more meals with the Mevans than anyone. They’re a good mix.

Meg is the starter. The invite to a meal or a party is most often from her. She comes loaded with researched recipes and ready to rock. All the contributors in this cookbook are close friends because of her. Meg’s desire to host bourbon tastings, kale cook-offs and front yard bonfires brought us all closer.

Evan is a lily-gilding culinary crazy man. He happily reads all the directions and then promptly disregards them. Evan often needs a kick in the pants to get started, but once folks are at his place, there is nothing he won’t do to make them happy and well fed. He literally turns into a grandmother demanding folks to “eat — eat, honey” and making sure no one’s glass gets below the halfway point.

Meg often goes to bed before the party ends while Evan stays up until the bitter end.

They are a good mix indeed. The Mevans love friends and sharing food with them. What better way to pay tribute to the couple than to collect some of the best recipes and stories from some of their best friends?
